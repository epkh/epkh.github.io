Is the Middle Class Left Out in Pennsylvania?

A Look at Middle Class Housing Burden in the Keystone State


Starting with the Great Recession and increasing with the 2016 Presidential Election, people across the United States started to realize that large parts of the US were being left out of political and social narratives.

With a large middle class, a history as a successful rustbelt state, and home to an increasingly polarized rural and urban population, Pennsylvania is a state that epitomizes these 21st century tensions.

Who is in the Middle Class?




This project examines the middle class in Pennsylvania, but who is a part of the middle class? The middle class can be defined in many ways. Here's how we define it... households are middle-income if they earn 2/3 to double median annual income, controlled by household size and year. The chart above shows how this is calculated for each year and household size we consider.*

Housing burden is a metric generally used with the most vulnerable households to determine what housing support they should receive from the government. But how is this middle class faring without the support of the government safety net? Are their costs going up faster than their incomes? Are middle class households in rural areas experiencing different housing burdens than those in urban areas?
